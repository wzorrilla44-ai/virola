02 / problem
The problem I solve
Small businesses are losing customers to competitors with better digital presence — but they don't have time or budget to fix it.
01
No follow-up system
A new gym member signs up, gets zero emails. They lose motivation and cancel. The gym has no idea why.
02
Inconsistent social media
They post twice in January, disappear for 6 weeks, wonder why their follower count doesn't grow.
03
Everything done manually
Booking confirmations, review requests, reminder texts — all done by hand. Hours wasted every week.
04
Agencies are too expensive
A local marketing agency charges $2K+/month. A solo operator like me can do the same work for a fraction.

05 / outreach
How I get clients
No cold calling. No ads. Just direct, human outreach on platforms they already use — with something real to show.
step 01
Find them on Instagram
Search local gym, barber, or restaurant accounts. Look for ones with low engagement or irregular posting — that's the problem I solve.
step 02
Send a short, specific DM
"Hey — I noticed you haven't posted in a few weeks. I help local gyms set up automated content and emails. I built a free sample — mind if I send it over?"
step 03
Offer a free trial week
Let them see real results before paying. Use the gym email sequence and Canva posters as proof of quality. Get a testimonial after.
step 04
Convert to a retainer
After the trial: "Want me to keep running this for $150/month?" — most say yes if the work is good. That's recurring revenue.
